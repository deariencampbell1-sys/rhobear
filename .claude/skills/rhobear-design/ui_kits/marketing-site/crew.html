SECTION · STORY · 02 / 20

Nine characters.
Each one is a role.

Not avatars. Not "personas". Nine real workers with a job each, on deck the moment you boot. They hand off to each other so you don't have to babysit a queue — and you can reshape, retheme, or grow the crew as far as you want.

Voice-first companion. Tap the mic, talk like a friend, no command syntax to learn.
ROLE · POPS

Your assistant.

Knows the codebase. Talks like a friend. First voice you hear when the hub boots: "Hello, Dearien. The crew is on deck." Pops keeps the room warm — and keeps receipts. Every command you spoke, every file the crew touched, rolled into the ship's log so nothing gets ghosted.

ROLE · ARCHITECT

Plans. Verifies.

Decomposes a goal into lanes, gates the work against the shape you asked for, and says no when the shape is wrong. Architect drafts the plan in your words first, then translates to ticketable tasks. The plan is editable — argue with it before the build starts, not after.

ROLE · FOREMAN

Builds. Ships PRs.

Heads-down builder. Reports back with proof — a diff, a passing test, a screenshot. Drags cards across the Board for you. Foreman runs in lanes, never on the same file as another worker, so two of them in parallel never wreck each other's edits.

ROLE · GUARDIAN

Reviews. Bounces.

Catches bugs before merge. Blocks sloppy changes with a reason — always specific, never vibes. Guardian reads the diff against the goal, against Architect's gate, and against the safety registry. Three checks before anything lands.

ROLE · SPARK

Generates media.

Runs video, image, and audio jobs while you sleep. Drop a brief, walk away, come back to a folder full of takes. Spark queues long jobs through the autoloop and surfaces previews when each one bakes — no babysitting the render.

ROLE · WIZARD

Pulls tools.

Searches the catalog, installs MCP servers, wires vetted business skills into the right agent — /triage-nda, /vendor-check, /compliance-check. The lookup desk for the crew. Wizard remembers which tool got the last job done and proposes it again next time — the registry learns the shape of your work.

A TYPICAL HANDOFF

The whole crew, one conversation, zero ticket-juggling.

pops > hello, dearien. boot sequence loaded. the crew is on deck. you > wire the worker card evidence into the visible tab architect > decomposing into 3 lanes [--gate strict] lane a · context rehydration guardian, ~8 min lane b · skill registry diff guardian + reviewer, ~12 min lane c · delivery manifest foreman, ~10 min foreman > opened PR #42 · +108 -15 · 2 files guardian > review-ready · 12 checks pass · 1 question for you wizard > tool used: mcp-fs.read_dir, mcp-shell.run
